Globally, a few big stories.

The Trump administration has proposed requiring all federal workers to sign non-disclosure agreements. The draft, published in the Federal Register, defines confidential government information extremely broadly, covering internal operations, personnel matters, and pre-decisional material. Workers who refuse to sign could be dismissed. Civil service unions are calling it a purge of career officials.

France had its hottest May day on record. Seven deaths have been linked to the heat in France alone. Scientists are describing temperatures as mind-bogglingly crazy for this time of year.

Also in Europe — a tragic accident in Belgium. A train struck a school minibus at a level crossing in Buggenhout on Tuesday, killing at least four people, including two children. The crossing barrier was closed and the red light was showing at the time.

In Serbia, tens of thousands clashed with riot police in Belgrade over the weekend in the latest surge of a student-led movement demanding early elections and an end to President Vucic's grip on power. The movement began after a deadly railway station collapse in Novi Sad eighteen months ago.
