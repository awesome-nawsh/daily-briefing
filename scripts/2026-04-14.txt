Here's your fact for today: exactly seventy years ago this morning, engineers at Ampex Corporation in Redwood City, California demonstrated the world's first practical videotape recorder. Before that moment, replaying a television broadcast meant filming the screen with a film camera. Within a year, the device was in every major broadcast studio. The rotating drum-head technology that made it work is still the core principle behind every modern hard drive. Not bad for a Tuesday in 1956.
