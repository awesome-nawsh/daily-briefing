Quick fun fact to kick things off: today is the one hundred and nineteenth birthday of Rachel Carson — the marine biologist who published Silent Spring in 1962, documenting how pesticides were silently wiping out bird populations and ecosystems across America. The chemical industry mounted a full-scale effort to discredit her before the book even hit shelves. It failed spectacularly. Silent Spring led directly to the US ban on DDT and is widely credited with launching the modern environmental movement. Not a bad legacy for a marine biologist from Pennsylvania.
