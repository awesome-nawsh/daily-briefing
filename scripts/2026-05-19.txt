Global Geopolitics

The Trump-Xi summit wrapped last Thursday in Beijing. The outcomes: China orders 200 Boeing jets, and Nvidia gets US clearance to sell H200 chips to major Chinese firms — a significant technology concession. Both sides agreed on a strategic stability framework for three years, with another summit this autumn. Taiwan was Xi's stated top priority — he reportedly warned Trump that mishandling it would put the whole relationship in great jeopardy. Cautious stabilisation, not a reset.

The Hormuz closure is now hitting European pocketbooks. Lufthansa has cut flights, EU governments are planning for fuel shortages, and Germany is releasing 19.5 million barrels from its strategic oil reserve. Analysts say the closure looks increasingly structural — military pressure alone has not reopened the waterway, and alternatives are being locked in.

In the UK, Keir Starmer is facing a genuine leadership crisis. Wes Streeting has mounted a formal challenge, and more than 97 Labour MPs are calling for him to go. UK gilt yields are at multi-decade highs.

Japan has approved arms sales to 17 countries — a major break from seven decades of constitutional pacifism, driven by Indo-Pacific security pressures.

Intelligence reports suggest a Russian vessel may have been transporting submarine nuclear reactors to North Korea. If confirmed, a serious new notch in that arms relationship.

Russia-Ukraine ceasefire talks remain stalled. Finland's Stubb is pushing for more direct EU engagement with Moscow.

Germany, South Africa, Australia

Germany's Defence Minister visited Rheinmetall's Australian plant with Canberra's defence industry minister — a clear sign of deepening European-Indo-Pacific supply chain integration. NATO European allies and Canada have lifted defence spending by a record 19.6 percent this year to 574 billion dollars.

South Africa: another deadly weekend in the Western Cape, with seven killed in mass shootings in Wesbank and Franschhoek. Gun Free SA is calling for urgent government action.

Australia unveiled its 2026-27 budget — tax cuts and resilience spending, with a heated political fight over the trust fund rules.
