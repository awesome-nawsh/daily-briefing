Good morning Sean, it's Wednesday, the fifteenth of April, 2026.

Today is the birthday of Leonardo da Vinci - born in Tuscany on this day in 1452. He had no formal education, and yet he filled more than 10,000 pages of notebooks with designs for flying machines, anatomical drawings, solar power concepts and hydraulic systems - most of them centuries ahead of anyone else. He never considered any field off-limits, and that is worth carrying into the day.

Over in Singapore, the Monetary Authority has tightened its monetary policy settings in response to yesterday's Q1 growth figures. The economy grew 4.6 percent year on year in the first quarter - solid on the surface - but the central bank flagged that growth will slow through the rest of the year. MAS raised its 2026 inflation forecast to between 1.5 and 2.5 percent, up from 1 to 2 previously. The driver is energy price risk from the US-Iran conflict, with oil holding above 100 dollars a barrel. The government also announced close to a billion Singapore dollars in support measures for households and businesses, with MAS responding by slightly increasing the appreciation rate of the Singapore dollar nominal effective exchange rate policy band.

Worth noting for travellers - Singapore Airlines is continuing to reroute its Asia-Europe services around closed Iranian airspace. Dubai, Riyadh and Jeddah services remain suspended. If you are flying Europe in the coming weeks, expect longer routes and check your itinerary.

On the global stage, the weekend's Iran talks collapsed without agreement. Vice President Vance had positioned Sunday's face-to-face negotiations as a breakthrough moment, but both sides walked away empty-handed. President Trump has since said the US will blockade the Strait of Hormuz, restricting maritime access to Iranian ports. With oil above 100 dollars and global shipping under pressure, this is the geopolitical story of the week.

And in Europe, Viktor Orban is out. After fourteen years in power, Hungary's Prime Minister lost Sunday's election to Peter Magyar, who founded his opposition Tisza party just two years ago. Voter turnout was the highest since the 1990s. European leaders were quick to congratulate Magyar - Orban's Hungary had been a consistent blocker of EU decisions on Ukraine funding, energy policy and immigration. It is a genuinely significant shift in the European political landscape.

On Germany - Chancellor Merz and President Zelenskyy sealed a sweeping defence pact in Berlin this week, covering drone co-production, data sharing and missile support. Ukraine gains German military backing; Germany gains access to Ukraine's battlefield-proven drone expertise. A practical deal at a practical moment.

In South Africa, the Democratic Alliance has elected Geordin Hill-Lewis as its new Federal Leader, following his tenure as Mayor of Cape Town. BOSA leader Mmusi Maimane said the result opens the door for a broader opposition coalition heading into local government elections, and announced a formal merger between BOSA and the People's Prosperity Movement under a unified banner. The opposition landscape is consolidating.

In Australia, Canberra has committed up to 15 billion dollars over the next decade to expand its drone capabilities. Australia will also be among 40 countries at an emergency summit in Paris next week, focused on coordinating a response to the Strait of Hormuz crisis.

On the tech front, Microsoft has launched three new foundational AI models: MAI-Transcribe-1 for speech-to-text, MAI-Voice-1 for audio generation, and MAI-Image-2 which extends into video. Microsoft is positioning itself as a full-stack AI model provider in direct competition with Google and OpenAI. Worth watching for enterprise adoption implications.

From the James Webb Space Telescope, scientists have identified what they are calling a forbidden planet - TOI-5205 b, a Jupiter-sized world orbiting a small cool star that current planetary science says should not be able to produce a gas giant. The planet has a detectable atmosphere, and it is forcing a rethink of how large planets form. The universe, once again, ignoring the textbooks.

For BHP, the company has completed its silver streaming agreement with Wheaton Precious Metals on the Antamina copper mine in Peru, receiving an upfront cash payment of 4.3 billion US dollars. Wheaton will receive 33.75 percent of Antamina's silver production at ongoing payments of 20 percent of spot price. It is a capital recycling move that keeps BHP's balance sheet focused on copper growth.

Quick one for the garage - the 2026 Ducati Monster Plus has arrived in its fifth generation with a new 890cc V-twin that sheds 13 pounds from the previous engine, bringing total bike weight to 386 pounds. Early ride reviews call it sharper, lighter and more engaging than ever.

Finally, Singapore today is warm and sunny - 29 degrees high, around 25 at the low end, with good sunshine hours as the inter-monsoon period settles in. Humidity around 90 percent, so dress light.
