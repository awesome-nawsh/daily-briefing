Good morning Sean, it's Thursday, the twenty-eighth of May.

Fun fact: all clownfish are born male. Every single one. They only become female when the dominant female in the group dies — which means Nemo's dad would technically have become Nemo's mum. Nature is wonderfully strange.

Your long weekend starts now. You have a stay at the Westin Desaru Coast Resort in Bandar Penawar through to Monday the first of June. Nothing else on the calendar. Well earned.

On Singapore. GastroBeats — the food and music festival — is making a comeback in June. And the Seng Choon egg farm is set to relocate after Lim Chu Kang land-use changes were announced, with four other farms also affected as Singapore reconfigures that agricultural corridor.

Globally, the dominant story is the US-Israeli war on Iran, now three months in. Iran's Strait of Hormuz closure has pushed oil past one hundred dollars a barrel — the biggest supply shock since the seventies — and the IEA has triggered an emergency release of strategic petroleum reserves. Ceasefire talks are inching forward, but the White House flatly denied an Iranian state TV report claiming a draft agreement was in place, calling it a complete fabrication. Markets are reacting unevenly: Japan's Nikkei briefly topped sixty-five thousand for the first time on Hormuz reopening hopes; South Korea's Kospi also hit a record high. Russia-Ukraine peace talks under US brokerage continue without a breakthrough. The EU has separately escalated with Moscow — Brussels called Russia's threats to foreign diplomats unacceptable, and Germany summoned the Russian Ambassador. On a longer timeline, analysts note China is quietly building its sanction-busting infrastructure — not for Iran, they say, but for any future Taiwan scenario.

Germany unveiled its World Cup squad this week, Neuer in goal with a blend of veterans and emerging talent. Berlin is also moving ahead with legislation requiring men and women over forty-five to do regular military exercises. In South Africa, Johannesburg's power debt crisis has a new proposed fix: ring-fencing city revenue and creating a direct Eskom to City Power partnership. In Australia, Labor is overhauling jobseeker welfare — the most significant reform in thirty years — easing mutual obligations and restructuring employment support.

In tech and AI. Google released Gemma 4, an open reasoning model under the Apache 2.0 licence. Meta announced eight thousand job cuts — ten percent of its workforce — as part of an AI restructuring, with seven thousand more staff reassigned to AI teams. The big platforms are reorganising fast. And ESPHome 2026.5.0 just dropped with a beta of the new Device Builder web app: proper configuration editor, firmware queues, bulk actions, distributed builds. Worth updating.

Science. NASA's Webb Telescope characterised a planet seven hundred light-years away with a precise daily weather pattern: silicate clouds form each morning and fully dissolve by nightfall. One of the most detailed exoplanet weather observations ever recorded.

And BHP. The Guardian and Four Corners published leaked internal documents showing BHP war-gamed delays to its decarbonisation commitments, and recently bought over five hundred million dollars in new diesel trucks for Jimblebar — despite earlier plans to electrify from 2027. Difficult optics. Expect scrutiny.

Motorcycles. Honda launched the WN7 — its first large-displacement electric bike — from January at around seventy-five hundred euros. Positioning it as a mainstream option, not a niche play.

Finally the weather. You're in Desaru today, but back in Singapore it's thirty-two degrees, humidity seventy-three percent, under five percent chance of rain. Beautiful conditions on both sides of the Causeway.

Have a brilliant long weekend Sean. Enjoy every minute of Desaru — back on Monday.
